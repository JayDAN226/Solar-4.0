Solar System

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. It includes eight planets, with four terrestrial planets in the inner Solar System and four gas giants in the outer Solar System. The terrestrial planets are Mercury, Venus, Earth, and Mars, while the gas giants are Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. There are also various other objects that orbit the Sun, including dwarf planets, asteroids, comets, and meteoroids. The Solar System developed 4.6 billion years ago from a molecular cloud, and it is constantly flooded by the Sun's charged particles, forming the heliosphere. The Solar System is located in the Milky Way galaxy and is constantly moving in its orbit around the Galactic Center.
